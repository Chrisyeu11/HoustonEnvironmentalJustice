What is Biodiversity?

Biodiversity, or biological diversity, refers to the variety of life on Earth at all its levels, from genes to ecosystems, and can encompass the evolutionary, ecological, and cultural processes that sustain life.

The Importance of Biodiversity

An explanation of why biodiversity is crucial for ecosystems, human well-being, and the balance of nature, including its role in ecosystem services, food security, and cultural significance.

Threats to Biodiversity

Discussing the main threats to biodiversity, such as habitat loss, climate change, invasive species, pollution, and overexploitation, and how these threats impact different species and ecosystems.

Conservation Efforts

Details about conservation efforts and strategies used to protect and restore biodiversity, including protected areas, legislation, and restoration ecology.

Endangered Species

Information on various species that are currently endangered, the risks they face, and the efforts being made to save them from extinction.

Biodiversity Hotspots

An overview of biodiversity hotspots — regions with significant levels of biodiversity that are under threat from humans — and why it's important to prioritize these areas for conservation efforts.

Highlighting how individuals and communities can contribute to biodiversity preservation through everyday actions, community science, and supporting local conservation initiatives.

A review of international agreements and global initiatives aimed at preserving biodiversity, such as the Convention on Biological Diversity and the goals of the Aichi Biodiversity Targets.
