TSU-Environmental Justice

Environmental justice is a social movement that works to protect the earth's ecosystems and address environmental inequalities. Texas Southern University (TSU) is a historically black private university located in Houston, United States, which is involved in various programs and activities related to environmental justice. TSU contributes to researching, supporting, and educating about environmental justice through the following methods: Research projects: TSU explores the best practices in emissions control, air and water pollution, and environmental health through academic and laboratory research on environmental justice. Courses and education: TSU provides knowledge about environmental justice to students in various disciplines, such as environmental science, urban and regional planning, public administration, and management at undergraduate and graduate levels. Collaboration: TSU works with schools, government agencies, non-profit organizations, and community organizations to ensure that environmental justice is integrated into overall development processes. Improving environmental sustainability on campus: TSU supports the development and adoption of sustainable strategies and technologies that take into account the three key components of the campus's environmental, economic, and social impacts. Universities like TSU are helping to address environmental inequalities in their communities by embracing academic efforts and practicing the principles of environmental justice and sustainability. These efforts are realized through education, research, and collaboration, and serve as a foundation for providing a sustainable environment for future generations.

About Us

Environmental justice, as a social movement, aims to ensure that all people have equal rights to a healthy environment, regardless of their race, socioeconomic status, or cultural background. This movement addresses environmental inequalities and works towards creating a sustainable and healthy environment for everyone

The movement raises public awareness about these disparities.
    • 2.Advocates for policy changes: Environmental justice groups and activists work with governments and policymakers to create and implement better environmental regulations that protect all communities. They encourage governments to consider the needs of overburdened communities in their environmental policies.
    • 3.Promotes community involvement: The movement supports the engagement of communities affected by environmental issues in decision-making processes. This ensures that their voices are heard, and their concerns are taken into consideration in developing policies and strategies.
    • 4.Encourages environmental cleanup and sustainable development: Environmental justice aims to rectify the damage caused by historical environmental inequalities. This includes cleaning up contaminated sites, addressing hazardous waste management, and promoting sustainable development practices that benefit all communities.
